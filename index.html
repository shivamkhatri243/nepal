World Heritage

Sacred Sites & Monuments

From the medieval durbar squares of the Kathmandu Valley to the living birthplace of the Buddha, Nepal's heritage sites are the architecture of a civilization.

UNESCO · Hindu

Pashupatinath Temple

The holiest Hindu shrine in Nepal, dating back to the 5th century. A sacred sanctuary on the banks of the Bagmati river.

UNESCO · Buddhist

Boudhanath Stupa

One of the largest Buddhist stupas in the world, its all-seeing eyes watch over Kathmandu Valley.

UNESCO · Ancient

Swayambhunath

The self-arisen stupa, crowning a hill above Kathmandu — among the oldest religious sites in Nepal.

Medieval · Newari

Patan Durbar Square

A masterpiece of Newari architecture — temples, courtyards and palaces of astonishing craftsmanship.

UNESCO · Birthplace of Buddha

Lumbini

The sacred birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, the Buddha — a pilgrimage site for millions worldwide.

Living Culture

Festivals & Celebrations

Nepal celebrates over 50 festivals a year. Each one is a vivid tapestry of devotion, community, music, and ancient ritual that has survived for millennia.

🪔
October / November

Tihar

The Festival of Lights — five days of illuminating homes with oil lamps and marigold garlands, honoring crows, dogs, cows, and siblings. Known as Nepal's Diwali.

🥁
October / November

Dashain

The grandest Hindu festival in Nepal. Fifteen days celebrating the goddess Durga's victory over evil, with kite flying, swings, buffalo sacrifices and family reunions.

🎭
August

Indra Jatra

An 8-day Kathmandu festival honoring the rain god Indra, famous for the chariot procession of the living goddess Kumari through the streets of the old city.

🌸
March / April

Holi

The joyous festival of colors. Streets erupt in vivid powders and water, celebrating the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil across the Terai and hills.

🏺
July / August

Rato Machhendranath

Patan's legendary chariot festival — the longest chariot festival in the world, lasting months. A Newari tradition ensuring rain and harvest for the valley.

⛰️
May / June

Buddha Jayanti

The birthday of Siddhartha Gautama celebrated with candles, processions and prayers in Lumbini, Swayambhunath and Boudhanath — a day of deep spiritual reflection.

Living Heritage

Ancient Traditions

Nepal's traditions are not museum pieces — they are alive, practiced daily in homes, temples and mountain communities across the country.

Kumari — The Living Goddess

A young Newari girl selected by ancient rites becomes the embodiment of the divine goddess Taleju, venerated by Hindus and Buddhists alike until she reaches puberty.

Namaste & the Art of Greeting

The pressed palms and bowed head carry a profound spiritual meaning: "I bow to the divine in you." It reflects Nepal's deep culture of reverence and humility.

Thanka Painting

Intricate scrolled paintings of Buddhist and Hindu deities, created with mineral pigments over weeks or months, serving as aids for meditation and devotional worship.

Mani Walls & Prayer Flags

In mountain communities, walls of carved mantra stones and fluttering prayer flags carry mantras into the wind — every gust a thousand prayers sent to the heavens.

Artisan Culture

Arts, Crafts & Music

Nepal's artistic traditions span 2,000 years of continuous practice — from intricate metalwork to devotional music that moves between temples and mountain winds.

🏺

Woodcarving

Extraordinarily detailed carvings on temple struts, windows, and peacock windows — a Newari art refined over centuries.

🪡

Pashmina Weaving

The finest cashmere in the world, handwoven in Nepal from Himalayan mountain goats — warm, lightweight and exquisite.

🎎

Metalwork & Statues

Lost-wax bronze casting and repousse metalwork — Patan's artisans have created divine statues for over a millennium.

🎵

Sacred Music

The madal drum, sarangi and bansuri flute form the heartbeat of Nepal's folk and devotional music tradition.

🧵

Dhaka Weaving

Geometric patterned cloth hand-woven in Palpa, used in traditional dress and gifted at auspicious occasions.

🏔️

Singing Bowls

Tibetan and Newari metal bowls that resonate with healing frequencies — used in meditation and sound therapy.

📜

Lokta Paper

Handmade from the bark of the lokta bush growing above 6,000 feet — Nepal's unique handmade paper craft.

💎

Gem Jewellery

Turquoise, coral, and semi-precious stones set in silver — worn by Tamang, Sherpa and hill communities for centuries.
